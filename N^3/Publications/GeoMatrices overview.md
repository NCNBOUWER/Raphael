# GeoMatrices overview

GeoMatrices is the live N^3 framework after the three-fascicle build. It is a scale-free ToE candidate framework and falsification architecture for closed systems.

## What GeoMatrices is

GeoMatrices is a closure-first system for representing, testing, and falsifying bounded structures. It combines:

1. finite probability and product-measure centrality,
2. barycentric and simplex closure,
3. Omnicompounded Barycentric Well geometry,
4. Multi-Observer Multi-Function Breakdown,
5. closure-preserving transport and covariant bridge language,
6. Raphael-facing crosswalks, equation exposure, and falsification routes.

## What GeoMatrices does not claim without more evidence

GeoMatrices does not claim empirical completion simply because a structure can be mapped. Domain claims require:

- a finite or normalisable component set,
- a closure/conservation rule,
- a coordinate or state-history sequence,
- a measurable residual or failure condition,
- domain-specific evidence, data, or qualified review.

Empirical domain validation begins at 200% only after external evidence is supplied. A second independent empirical method can lift the claim to 250%.
