## Role of Fascicle III

Fascicle III is the **Raphael integration layer**. It must use the mathematics of Fascicle I and the architectural/classical analysis of Fascicle II to support, constrain, crosswalk, or falsify fixed Raphael equation claims.

Fascicle III does not make Raphael by rewriting it. It tests and organises Raphael against the already built N^3 proof stack.

## Hard constraints

- Raphael equations are fixed and source-governed.
- Raphael Lite is not built until Fascicles I-III are complete and the final Raphael_Physics folder is ready.
- Fascicle III should not re-prove Fascicle I or II; it imports their results.
- Fascicle III is where Raphael-specific integration occurs, but physical claims must still carry proof status.
- EML Function is in `EML#F`, not EM Function, EMFF, or RFS.
- Files use clean names without version strings unless the file is an internal historical record.
